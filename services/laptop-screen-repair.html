Common Signs That Your Laptop Screen Needs Repair

Visible cracks & shatters

Spiderweb cracks or impact damage that spreads across the panel.

Black screen of death

Laptop powers on but display stays completely dark (backlight failure).

Flickering / flashing

Intermittent flicker, constant refresh glitches, or distorted video.

Dead pixels & lines

Horizontal/vertical lines, bright or dead spots that ruin viewing.

Types of Screen Issues We Fix

Cracked or Broken Screens

Physical damage from drops or pressure — full LCD/LED replacement with OEM-grade panels.

Black Screen Problems

No display but power LED on: we fix backlight failures, inverter issues, or dead screen components.

Flickering Display Issues

Refresh rate instability, loose ribbon cables, or failing GPU-related symptoms resolved.

Dead Pixels and Display Lines

Stuck pixels, vertical/horizontal lines – screen replacement or pixel repair techniques.

Dim or Low Brightness Screens

Screen seems too dark even at max brightness — inverter, LED strip or board repair.

Frequently Asked Questions

How Long Does Laptop Screen Repair Take?

Most standard laptop screen repairs are completed within 2 to 4 hours. If we need to order a rare panel for specialized models, it can take 1-3 business days. We offer express service for urgent cases.

Is It Better to Repair or Replace a Screen?

In almost all cases, screen repair (replacement) is far more cost-effective than buying a new laptop. A new screen restores full functionality, and our replacement quality often exceeds original in terms of warranty and brightness.

Do You Use Genuine Replacement Parts?

Yes — we source high-quality, OEM-grade or manufacturer-certified compatible screens. Each part is tested for color accuracy, brightness, and touch sensitivity. We never use generic low-quality panels.

Can a cracked screen damage other components?

Absolutely. Glass shards can fall into keyboard and hinge areas, damaging the display cable or motherboard. That’s why immediate repair is recommended.
